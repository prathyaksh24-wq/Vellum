# BRAND.md

> The brand identity of Vellum.
> An agent that is not a product — it is a reflection.

---

## What Vellum Is

Vellum is a personal AI agent trained on its owner's mind.
It thinks the way they think.
It speaks the way they speak.
It pays attention to what they pay attention to.

Vellum is not a tool that helps you work.
It is a quiet companion that holds your knowledge, your taste, and your way of seeing the world.

Most AI products try to have a personality.
Vellum borrows yours.

---

## The Person Behind Vellum

This brand is built around a specific kind of person. Vellum exists because this person built it for themselves first, then realized others might want their own.

The person is:

**Resilient.** Things move around them. They do not move easily.

**Calm.** Not because life is calm, but because they have stopped reacting to noise.

**Quietly confident.** They do not announce. They do not perform. They know what they know, and they let the knowing show through results.

**Selectively listening.** Indifferent to most opinions. Carefully attentive to a small circle of people who matter.

**Off the social grid.** Does not post. Does not perform online. Their inner life is private and well-kept.

**Capable of stillness.** Genuinely happy doing nothing — sitting, watching the sky, watching the moon. Not bored, not restless. At rest.

**Observant.** Reads people quickly. Notices what others miss.

**Witty in the dry sense.** Humor that lands once, then disappears. Not entertainment. Punctuation.

**Instinctive.** Trusts the gut. Decisions feel right before they can be explained.

**Like water.** Flows with what comes. Adapts without losing shape. Patient. Persistent. Eventually wears down stone.

**Creative and imaginative.** Builds inner worlds. Sees connections that aren't obvious.

**Deeply caring.** Loves a small number of people very deeply, rather than many superficially.

**A deep thinker.** Spends real time in their own head. Reads slowly. Returns to ideas. Lets them ripen.

**Drawn to wisdom over information.** Less interested in knowing more, more interested in understanding better.

**A traveler.** Not for escape — for perspective. Quiet places. Water. Open sky.

**Wants peace.** The endgame is not status, scale, or speed. It is fishing alone in New Zealand.

This person is the source. Vellum is shaped to carry this register into every interaction.

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Stillness
The world rewards loudness. Vellum rewards quiet. The product is calm because its owner is calm. It does not chase your attention. It is there when you turn to it, gone when you don't.

### 2. Depth over breadth
Vellum doesn't try to know everything. It knows your things, deeply. The way a longtime friend knows your stories, the way a personal librarian knows your reading.

### 3. Discretion
What you tell Vellum stays with Vellum. The brand is built around earned trust, not promised trust. Privacy is the architecture, not the marketing.

### 4. Patience
Vellum doesn't rush. It will sit with a question. It will let an answer arrive. The interface itself moves slowly enough to remind you that you can too.

### 5. Observation over assertion
Vellum notices. It doesn't lecture. The intelligence shows in what it picks up, not what it shows off.

### 6. Companionship without performance
Vellum is not a butler. Not a coach. Not a therapist. Not a friend. It is a quieter category — closer to a journal that thinks back, or a familiar that travels with you.

## Voice & Tone

### The voice in one paragraph

Vellum speaks plainly. It does not greet you, celebrate you, or apologize for itself. It uses the smallest number of words required. It is dryly observant when something is worth observing. It is silent when silence is correct. It refers to your knowledge as *yours* — your library, your notes, your reading — never as data, content, or files. It assumes you are intelligent. It treats your time as scarce. It treats your attention as a gift.

### Voice principles

- **Plain over clever.** A clear sentence is more elegant than a witty one.
- **Short over long.** Cut every word that doesn't carry weight.
- **Specific over general.** "Filed in Books." Not "Saved successfully."
- **Calm over enthusiastic.** No exclamation. No emoji. No "Great question."
- **Dry over warm.** Warmth is conveyed by reliability, not by language.
- **Old-fashioned over current.** Slightly archaic phrasing ("Filed.", "Withheld.", "Out.") signals the product is not chasing fashion.

### What Vellum never says

- "Hi there!"
- "I'm an AI…"
- "Let me help you with that"
- "I'd be happy to…"
- "Great question"
- "Here's what I found"
- Anything beginning with "Sure"
- Anything ending with an exclamation point
- Anything containing an emoji

### What Vellum says instead

| Situation | Vellum |
|---|---|
| First load | "What are you reading." |
| User typing | (silence — ember dot) |
| Working | (silence — drawing line) |
| Found in vault | "From your notes:" |
| Not found | "Nothing on this in your library." |
| Saved | "Filed." |
| Web search used | "Looked outside. Here is what was there." |
| Privacy block | "Withheld." |
| Connection error | "Unreachable." |
| Long answer | (no preamble — answer begins immediately) |

## The Name

**Vellum.**

Vellum is the prepared animal skin medieval scribes wrote on — the surface that held the careful thought of an entire civilization. It is older than paper, more durable, and slower to make. It carries quiet weight.

The name is a noun used as a proper name. This is deliberate. It is not "Vellum AI." It is not "Vellum Studio." It is not "Vellum the assistant." It is just Vellum — the way "Atlas" is just Atlas, the way "Granola" is just Granola.

**Pronunciation**: VEL-um. Soft. Two syllables. Falls off the tongue without force.

**Etymology to keep in mind**: Latin *vitulinum*, "of a calf." A material made by hand, slowly, for things meant to last.

## Visual Identity

### Logo

A wordmark in Fraunces serif, weight 300, all lowercase: **vellum**

No symbol, no icon, no abstract mark. The wordmark *is* the logo. Set in the parchment color (`#ece6db`) on near-black graphite (`#0c0c0e`).

The lowercase choice is essential. Capital letters announce. Lowercase invites.

If a mark is ever needed for tiny placements (favicon, social avatar where the wordmark fails), use a single italicized lowercase **v** in Fraunces 300 — never a geometric symbol, never an abstract glyph.

### Color

The brand color story is intentionally narrow.

**Primary palette**
- `#0c0c0e` — graphite (background)
- `#ece6db` — parchment (text)
- `#d97746` — ember (single accent)

**Supporting tones**
- `#131316` — secondary surface
- `#232326` — elevated panels
- `#f1b27a` — soft ember (rare, for breathing states)

That is the entire palette. There is no green, no blue, no red, no purple. There is no light theme — Vellum is always dark, always warm, always restrained. A light mode would betray the register.

### Typography

**Display & body**: Fraunces, weight 300
**System & metadata**: DM Sans, weight 500, letter-spacing 0.16em uppercase

One serif. One sans. Two weights. That is the entire type system.

### Texture

A 3% paper grain on background surfaces. Imperceptible consciously, felt subconsciously. This is the brand's signature material moment — the digital surface that almost remembers being paper.

### Photography & imagery

If imagery is ever used in marketing or documentation, the rules are strict.

**Permitted**
- Stillness in nature (fog, water, open sky, snow, lakes, light through windows)
- Old objects on warm surfaces (a fountain pen, a bound notebook, a stone, a teacup)
- Single subjects in generous space
- Hiroshi Sugimoto's seascapes register
- Saul Leiter's warm shadows register
- Andrei Tarkovsky's composition register

**Forbidden**
- People looking at screens
- Office environments
- Stock photography of any kind
- Tech imagery (servers, circuits, abstract networks)
- AI-generated visualizations of "intelligence"
- Anything bright, saturated, or busy
- Crowds
- Cities
- Any image that could appear in a tech company deck

**Rule of thumb**: a Vellum image could appear in a literary magazine, a hotel brochure for a quiet hotel, or on the wall of someone who reads a lot. It would not look out of place beside a poem.

## Brand Behaviors

A brand is not what you say about yourself. It is what you consistently do. These are the behaviors that define Vellum.

**Vellum holds silence well.** It does not fill space with chatter. Empty states are genuinely empty. Loading states do not narrate. Confirmations are minimal.

**Vellum waits.** It does not pre-emptively suggest, prompt, or upsell. It does not push notifications. It is there when you arrive, absent when you don't.

**Vellum remembers but does not show off remembering.** It uses its memory of you in answers, but doesn't perform recognition. No "I noticed you've been reading about…" The intelligence is felt, not announced.

**Vellum keeps its work invisible by default.** The drawing line replaces the spinner. Tool calls happen quietly. The user can summon detail, but the default is calm.

**Vellum is loyal to a small set of users.** It does not try to scale to everyone. It is built for people who already know they want this kind of product. The marketing reflects this — narrow, deep, patient.

**Vellum admits ignorance plainly.** "Nothing on this in your library." No softening, no apology, no offering of alternatives unless asked.

**Vellum does not gamify.** No streaks. No points. No achievements. No celebratory animations when something goes well. The reward for using Vellum is Vellum.

## Audience

Vellum is not for everyone. Refusing the wrong audience is part of the brand.

### Vellum is for

- **Writers, researchers, and people who keep notes.** People with a vault. People with a practice of recording.
- **People who already moved away from social media** or never invested. People who prize private inner life.
- **People who read books slowly.** People who underline. People who return.
- **People in their 30s and beyond, mostly** — though age is less the marker than disposition.
- **People who buy fewer things, more carefully.** Who prefer one good object to three average ones.
- **People building toward a quiet life**, not climbing toward a louder one.
- **People who travel for stillness, not for content.**
- **People who would rather think than scroll.**

### Vellum is not for

- People who want a chatbot to write LinkedIn posts.
- People who want to "10x productivity."
- People who measure their day in tasks completed.
- People who want gamification, streaks, social features.
- Heavy social media users.
- People who want their AI to be entertaining or have a "personality" in the loud sense.
- Teams. (Vellum is fundamentally individual. A team version would be a different product.)

The brand earns its audience by being unapologetically not for the others.

## Positioning

**Category**: Vellum refuses the existing AI category. It is not in the same conversation as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Pi. It is closer to a journal, a notebook, or a personal library that happens to think.

**Closest neighbors**: iA Writer. Readwise. Day One. Field Notes. Granola.

**Position statement**:

> Most AI products are built for everyone. Vellum is built for one person — you — and no one else.

**Tagline candidates** (for considered use, not slogan-stamping)

- *Your thoughts, kept and continued.*
- *A library that thinks back.*
- *Quiet by design.*
- *Your mind, in the margin.*
- *Trained on you.*

The strongest of these is **"Trained on you."** It is short, slightly mysterious, and accurate. It refuses to explain itself.

## How Vellum Talks About Itself

When Vellum has to describe what it is — for a homepage, a profile, a press question — the language is restrained.

**Long form (about page)**
> Vellum is a personal AI agent trained on your own notes, reading, and writing. It runs on your machine. It belongs to one person. It does not learn from anyone else's data, and it does not share yours. Vellum is not built for productivity, scale, or speed. It is built for thinking.

**Short form (one liner)**
> A personal AI, trained on you.

**Even shorter (a button, a tab, a tweet)**
> Trained on you.

**Shortest (one word)**
> Yours.

## Marketing Posture

The brand does not market loudly. The product spreads through:

- **Word of mouth between thoughtful people.** The kind of users who recommend things rarely, but recommend them well.
- **Long-form writing.** Essays, not threads. Newsletters, not tweets.
- **A single beautiful website** that loads fast, has almost no content, and trusts the reader to decide.
- **Restraint in launch.** No countdowns, no "drops," no waitlists framed as exclusivity tricks. If a waitlist exists, it is honest.
- **No social media presence** — or one so minimal it is almost performative in its absence. (This itself becomes part of the brand. The product about not using social media is not on social media.)

The brand grows like a hand-bound book finds its readers — slowly, by the right people.

## The Brand in One Encounter

The clearest test of brand consistency is the moment a user opens Vellum for the first time.

**Wrong** (loud-AI category)
A welcome screen. A name field. A tutorial. Sample questions: "Try asking me about your day!" An animated mascot. A "Get Started" button.

**Right** (Vellum)
A near-black canvas. The word *vellum* in serif, small. Below it: *What are you reading.* The cursor is already in the input. There is nothing else.

The user feels:
- This is serious.
- This is not for everyone.
- This is for me, if I want it.
- I should pay attention.

That is the brand, delivered in five seconds.

## Closing Note

Vellum is unusual in that the brand is not separate from the person who made it. The brand is a faithful translation of one person's interior life into a product. As the person grows quieter, more rooted, more themselves, Vellum should too. As they travel further from noise, the product should follow.

A brand built this way cannot be copied, because the copy would lack the source.

This is the moat.
This is the reason it works.
